Founder Playbook

Founder Mindset

  • Stay obsessively close to the problem.
  • Do things that do not scale early on.
  • Default to action and fast learning loops.

Choosing Co-Founders

  • Complementary strengths and trust.
  • Alignment on speed, ambition, and values.
  • Clear equity, roles, and decision rights.

Idea Selection

  • Start with a real pain point you understand.
  • Focus on small, intense user segments.
  • Find leverage where a small team can win.

Product Doctrine

Product is the engine. Growth and scale are outcomes of lovable, useful, and repeatable value.

Build Something People Love

  • Ship a simple version fast.
  • Watch users struggle; fix what hurts.
  • Make the core loop delightful and reliable.

Prototyping

  • Prototype to learn, not to impress.
  • Validate the main workflow first.
  • Reduce risk with weekly iterations.

Product-Market Fit

  • Listen for pull, not just push.
  • Track retention and repeat usage.
  • Refine positioning to match reality.

Operating Cadence

Weekly Rhythm

  • Ship one meaningful improvement each week.
  • Talk to at least five users.
  • Review metrics that reflect user value.

Monthly Review

  • Assess product-market fit indicators.
  • Audit growth channels and conversion.
  • Decide on pivots or focus expansions.

Quarterly Planning

  • Set one big goal and three key bets.
  • Align team on roadmap and constraints.
  • Revisit mission, vision, and culture.
