Course 1

The Kingdom Of Heaven

A study journey inspired by Dr. Myles Munroe's "Rediscovering the Kingdom" that reframes leadership, citizenship, and mission through the lens of God's original plan to extend heaven's culture on earth.

Focus: Leadership, dominion, and kingdom culture Format: 4 parts, guided reflection, mission practice Outcome: Live as sons, citizens, and ambassadors

Core Affirmations

These affirmations set the tone for the course. They anchor the learner to the purpose of Jesus and the restoration of kingdom identity.

Purpose

  • The purpose of Jesus was a kingdom on earth in the hearts of men.
  • The message of Jesus was the kingdom of heaven on earth.

Identity

  • God never planned for subjects but for sons.
  • Citizens and ambassadors represent a kingdom, not a religion.

Restoration

  • Jesus came to restore kings who lost their rulership.
  • The kingdom is not a place only, it is a functioning people.

Course Outline

The course is organized into four movements. Each part builds a kingdom worldview and equips learners to lead from heaven's standards.

Part 1: Discovering Your Personal Kingdom Leadership

Original plan and strategy, creator and rulership, prayer as kingdom protocol, and the mission to transform followers into leaders.

Part 2: The Dominion Mandate of Leadership

Kingdom government, dominion as stewardship, and the standard-setting role of the King with his sons as culture keepers.

Part 3: Contrasts in Kingdoms

Repentance as a mindset shift, identity as kingdom, and the power of knowledge to drive freedom and leadership.

Part 4: The Kingdom Assignment of Jesus

Mission statement, objectives, and measurable outcomes for restoring citizens and ambassadors to kingdom life.

Kingdom Concepts

A kingdom is shaped by its king. The greater the king, the greater the kingdom. Learners explore the standards of heaven and the mandate to mirror them on earth.

Creator and ruler Dominion and stewardship Citizenship and ambassadorship Prayer as authorization Leadership by identity
"Without God, we cannot. Without us, God will not." - John Wesley. Prayer becomes the authorization channel for heaven's resources to reach earth.

Mission Assignment of Jesus

Mission

  • Bring back the kingdom of God to earth.
  • Restore a family of sons and a commonwealth of citizens.

Objectives

  • Cleanse man as the temple to welcome the Governor.
  • Reconnect fallen ambassadors to the Government.
  • Activate citizens in kingdom living.

KPIs

  • Fallen sons cleansed by the finished work of the cross.
  • Holy Spirit breathed into disciples for mission.
  • Sons effectively carrying the Father's business on earth.

Weekly Practice

Each week pairs study with action: read, reflect, and deploy your leadership in daily assignments. The goal is to make the planet look like heaven through kingdom culture.

Read

  • Study the kingdom narrative and key scriptures.
  • Note the standards set by the King.

Reflect

  • Identify where religion replaced relationship.
  • Renew the mind around sons, citizens, and ambassadors.

Deploy

  • Bring kingdom order to your work and home.
  • Lead with stewardship, mastery, and service.

Ready to begin?

Start with Part 1 and track your progress through the four movements. Keep a journal to document how your mindset shifts and how kingdom culture grows in your domain.
